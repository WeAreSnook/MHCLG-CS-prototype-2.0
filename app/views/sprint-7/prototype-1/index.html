Cyber health framework

Welcome to the Cyber Health Framework for local authorities in England. This framework has been developed by MHCLG to support local authorities to:

  • Understand the levels of cyber security they should be reaching
  • Determine their own organisationâ€™s level of cyber security
  • Devise a path to build greater resilience
  • Achieve their PSN and NHS DSTP compliance
  • See how they map against other common standards, including Cyber Essentials, Cyber Essentials + and ISO 27001

Progression levels

The framework includes three levels of cyber health progression, and is designed to encourage continuous cyber improvement. The three levels are:

  • Level 1 (Baseline): this is the minimum level that all local authorities should be meeting.
  • Level 2 (Target): this level includes additional controls that local authorities should put in place to mitigate against more technically capable cyber attacks.
  • Level 3 (Advanced): this level is encouraged for local authorities facing the most advanced cyber or network and information security threats, or those providing the most essential public services.

Each progression levels maps to specific compliance processes or standards, which means by achieving each progression level you will also be meeting those requirements:

  • Level 1: Cyber Essentials, Cyber Essentials +
  • Level 2: PSN and IT Health Checks, PCI DSS, GDPR Security Outcomes
  • Level 3: ISO 2700, NIS-CAF
