You have 5 high risks

Topic Risk Action
Back up policies & procedures The council does not have a known working backup, at a secondary or centrally accessible site. If an extreme event impacts the primary location then the integrity or the availability of the Council's critical information is compromised, resulting in reduced chance of successful business continuity or disaster recovery. Update
Mobile Media/ Devices Some of the Council's mobile devices, tablets and/or laptops do not encrypt stored information at rest. If a device were lost or stolen, then a third-party may compromise sensitive information stored on the device resulting in reputational damage for your Council and the liklihood of ICO enforcement or fines. Update
Software assets register & management Some of the Council's software applications are no longer supported by the vendor. If unsupported software is used, then unpatched vulnerabilities will be present that could be exploited by third party attackers, resulting in loss of the confidentiality, availability and integrity of the Council's information and/or applications. Update
Secure configuration Software based firewalls are not enabled on the Council's PC and laptop endpoint devices. If a device is used on a network that includes an untrusted device (e.g. hotel WiFi or a home network) then the Council's device could be exposed to network based attacks, resulting in the loss of integrity and availability of the device and information held within it. Update
Boundary/Firewall management One or more of the Council's firewalls can be remotely administered by IT staff, using a login function from the public internet. If a remote attacker discovers the login page, they may launch brute force or dictionary attacks that could compromise login credentials, resulting in loss of integrity of the Councils firewall and potential compromise of the entire network. Update
